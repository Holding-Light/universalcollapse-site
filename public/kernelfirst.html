This is the program’s entry point. It is meant to be read before the domain papers (WP01–WP05) develop the kernel in physics, biology, and mind.

Kernel-first, not primitive-first

Universal Collapse Theory is easily misread if it is forced into the familiar pattern of primitive-first metaphysics — matter-first, mind-first, information-first, mathematics-first — or if its own structural vocabulary is taken to crown structure itself as that base.

UCT claims neither. It begins with structured possibility, active constraint, resolution, record, and update. Collapse is not a thing in the universe, a hidden force, or a replacement physical mechanism. It is a formal term for constrained actualization: the transition by which an admissible possibility space resolves into an actual state under operative constraints, leaving records that alter future possibility.

The Observer’s Fallacy

The paper’s central diagnostic is the base-layer reflex: promoting some nameable layer into the role of grounding all the others, when every layer examined turns out to be constrained by a wider context. UCT’s commitment is that no level we reach grounds the rest, and what is out of reach is neither crowned as a base nor denied.

This is why UCT is not a Theory of Everything — not in the physicist’s unification sense, and not in the metaphysical “everything is X” sense. It is a substrate-neutral structural grammar for making domains mutually legible without reducing them to a single base layer.

What would make it wrong

A gateway paper should prevent overclaim as much as misreading. Section 9 separates the kinds of claims involved — terminological, formal, negative metaphysical — and states the status of each. If you read one section of this paper before deciding whether to read the rest, read that one.
